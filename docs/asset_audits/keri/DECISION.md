# Keri Phase 2A Decision

Date: 2026-07-14

## Decision

CONDITIONAL GO TO PHASE 2B

## Gate Question

Can Keri move to Phase 2B - Full-body Leg Extension Proof?

Yes, conditionally. Keri has enough source, license/provenance evidence, body continuity, and short-bottom/skirt support to justify one controlled Phase 2B proof. Keri is not approved as the final anchor.

## Conditions

- Do not import assets into Godot until Phase 2B explicitly starts.
- Use only one selected body candidate for the first proof.
- Preserve the upper body; extend only missing lower legs, ankles, feet, and required canvas area.
- Normalize to the project canvas deliberately after extension.
- Treat long pants as deferred or extension-required.
- Use skirt or shorts for immediate bottom proof if a bottom is needed.
- Keep Konett attribution and LunaLucid/Namastaii provenance notes with any future proof.
- Keep PSD hierarchy as partially unknown unless a PSD-capable tool is used later.
- Do not treat Keri as a final product anchor without a later acceptance gate.

## Decision Factors

| Factor | Result |
|---|---|
| Body candidate quality | Good upper-body candidate, but lower legs/feet missing. |
| Crop information for outpaint | Sufficient for a proof because thighs/lower-shin direction remains visible. |
| Resolution | Usable for proof, but requires canvas normalization from `948x1920` to project target. |
| Short bottom or skirt usability | Yes; shorts and skirt are likely usable after body extension. |
| Long pants | Not usable as-is; can be deferred or extended later. |
| Face proof outlook | Medium; face parts exist, but nose/base face and hair separation need later work. |
| License/provenance | Sufficient for proof with attribution and version caveats. |
| PSD unknowns | Not blocking for Phase 2B because exported template PNGs are sufficient for body proof. |
| Approx. five assets per category | Realistic only after body proof; dress, shoes, and accessories need new work. |

## Phase 2B Scope If Started Later

- One selected body/base.
- Lower-leg, ankle, and foot extension.
- Canvas expansion/normalization.
- Skin tone, line art, and shading match pass.
- One visual check with a skirt or shorts candidate.
- No face replacement, no wardrobe expansion, no long-pants completion unless separately scoped.

## Later MVP Scope Update

After this Phase 2A decision, the MVP scope was rebaselined away from full-body leg extension. The historical audit decision above remains unchanged, but the next MVP phase is now documented in `MVP_SCOPE_DECISION.md` as `GO TO THREE-QUARTER-BODY MVP INTEGRATION`.
